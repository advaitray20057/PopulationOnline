How is population growth slowing down

Population growth slowdown may be good now but it will affect us in the long run

Advait Ray

24 July 2022

As mentioned in the main page, the World population growth rate is 0.84% which is less than half of what it was 60 years ago at 2.1%. Now you may be wondering how did this happen and why. Firstly and the most important of all is a decline in the total fertility rate (TFR). The fertility rate is the average number children born per woman. Globally this number is 2.4 but it varies from a high of 6.6 in Niger to a low of 1.1 in South Korea. Due to this huge variation the UN has set a replacement level for the TFR which is 2.1. Most developed countries have a TFR lower than 2.1 like the US at 1.8, the UK at 1.7, and Japan at a shockingly low 1.4 But you may be wondering how did this happen. Well firstly urbanization resulted in people having busier lives which meant not a lot of time to take care of children. Secondly, people were living longer. The life expectancy has increased from 53.1 years in 1962 to 71.7 years in 2022 and that meant that you can deliver babies later in life but that meant you could only have one or two children. The third and final reason is COVID-19 and that's because of the lockdowns because the parents could only take care of them because nobody could go outside. UNFPA 2022, World Population Prospects 2022
